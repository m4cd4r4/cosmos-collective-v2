## Orbital mechanics honesty

Real periods/eccentricities feed a genuine Kepler solver (Newton's method, 3392), but: distances hand-compressed non-linearly (Mercury 20.7 units/AU down to Pluto 2.1 - roughly `16 * r_AU^0.43`), all orbits coplanar, perihelion orientations ignored (mean longitude used as mean anomaly, 2554-2563 - Mercury ~20 deg off), positions decoupled from the displayed date. Planets are also ~900-1500x oversized relative to orbits. The scene is a toy space - which is exactly why the game build adopted it as such.

## JWST integration verdict (parked)

The solar system shows where JWST IS; the JWST feature shows what it SEES. Respect the asymmetry.

- **Do (when resumed):** JWST marker at Sun-Earth L2 inside the iframe (exaggerated anti-sunward offset, dashed tether, halo ellipse, self-contained info card copied - not imported - from `src/data/spacecraft-catalog.ts`); bidirectional deep links (`?focus=jwst` in, `postMessage` out with a no-parent guard); later, the sunshield 85-135 deg field-of-regard annulus.
- **Avoid:** embedding JWSTViewer/JWSTSkyMap on the solar system page; full merge; live pointing beams before the app stops showing wrong numbers.
- **Hard precondition for any cross-promotion:** fix the JWST feature's data integrity - Hubble comparison pairs show wrong objects in nearly every case (Neptune vs Saturn, Carina vs Eagle) and several "MIRI" images are different targets entirely. This violates the site's "real data, never fake it" principle today.
